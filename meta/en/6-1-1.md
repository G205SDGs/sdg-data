The legal basis for water supply in Germany is regulated by federal level in the <a href="https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/whg_2009/" target="_blank" onclick="return confirm_alert('the Federal Ministry of Justice','En');">Federal Water Resources Act</a> (Wasserhaushaltsgesetz, WHG) and at the state level in the respective <a href="https://www.saarheim.de/Gesetze_Laender/wasserg_laender.htm" target="_blank" onclick="return confirm_alert('Saarheim','En');">Water Laws of the LÃ¤nder</a>. Water supply is a public service and is usually provided by local authorities. The obligation to connect to and use the public water supply is intended to ensure a secure supply of drinking water to the population and to prevent or restrict the private use of groundwater. Over 99% of the population in Germany is connected to central drinking water distribution networks.
